Albert Einstein


Albert Einstein was a theoretical physicist known for his contributions to the theory of relativity. He was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany, and passed away on April 18, 1955, in Princeton, New Jersey, USA.

Einstein's famous equation, E=mcÂ², demonstrates the relationship between energy and mass, laying the foundation for modern physics. His theories of special and general relativity transformed our understanding of space, time, and gravity.



Scientific Achievements

  • Published the theory of special relativity in 1905.
  • Formulated the theory of general relativity in 1915.
  • Received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his work on the photoelectric effect.
  • Contributed to the development of quantum mechanics.

Humanitarian Work


Beyond his scientific pursuits, Einstein was a vocal advocate for peace, civil rights, and social justice. He used his platform to address issues such as nuclear disarmament and racial equality.
